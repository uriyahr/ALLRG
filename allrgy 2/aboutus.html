Uriyah Ruiz, an incoming senior at EVHS is now using her skills in languages to help her community in what she thinks is important to her. Having a nephew with many allergies, she notices the difficulties her sister has to find food out of home to eat. Uriyah took initiative to help her nephew and others with their problem.

Riya Chandra is a rising junior at The Harker School. She applied to Girls Who Code because she wanted to solidify her skills that she learned in AP Computer Science and meet other girls who have a similar interests. At school, Riya is a VP of her DECA Chapter where she competes in various business events. In her free time, Riya likes to dance and try new food at different restaurants. Riya has a strong connection to allergies as her cousin and best friend are both allergic to dairy and eggs. She found that both of them have such a hard time finding menu items suitable to them so she became very interested in building this product to change the lives of people who have allergies.


























































Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program

We met at the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program 2015. The Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program is a 7-week intensive computer science course that embeds classrooms in technology companies and universities. Girls learn everything from robotics to mobile development to HTML and CSS while gaining exposure to the tech industry and receiving valuable mentorship from women working in technology.
